The Business Case

Technically Feasibility

Hardware

Welio and a typical smartphone have nearly identical hardware requirements:

  • Cameras
  • GPS
  • WiFi
  • Cellular
  • Bluetooth
  • Accelerometer
  • Battery

You can think of the main device as a screenless smartphone with wide angle cameras. We can lean heavily on the work that has already been done to quickly and efficiently develop this product. In fact, the recently announced Android Things IoT platform would allow us to leverage existing Android development tools and developers.


Software - IoT Platforms

There are many platforms made specifically for IoT device/data management. Some potential options to power Welio include:

  • Microsoft Azure IoT
  • Google Weave
  • IBM Watson IoT
  • AWS IoT
  • Particle Cloud

In addition, there are several companies that offer platforms specifically designed for connected vehicle applications. They offer a white label software platform with a mobile app that is designed to interact with 3G/GPS enabled OBD2 transmitters (no standard video/image support).

  • Mojio Open Connected Car Platform
  • Agnik Connected Vehicle Platform
  • Vinli Cloud Platform

Connectivity

Cellular carriers are receptive to IoT companies

  • AT&T
  • Verizon

Welio will be designed to limit the amount of cellular data consumed. Video is only transmitted if an incident occurs. The locally stored video can easily be reviewed/downloaded on the users phone via an ad-hoc connection.


Summary

There has never been a better time to create this product. The hardware is freely available and economical, the infrastructure for efficiently managing IoT devices is established, and cellular carriers are actively engaged with supporting IoT companies.

Market Opportunity

While there are multiple companies competing in this space, the vast majority of them use very similar hardware (OBD2 reader with GPS/3G). There is currently no product on the market that is standalone and with a comprehensive feature set.

Welio differentiates itself in part by integrating cameras and an accelerometer to create a compelling all-in-one package that will appeal to many market segments, including:

  • Car enthusiasts - access driving data compiled from the GPS, Camera, Accelerometer, and OBD2
  • Parents - easily monitor their kids driving behavior
  • Technophiles - turns any car into a ‘smart car’
  • Safety conscious - automated crash reporting, drowsiness detection, and incident recording
  • Rideshare/commercial drivers - easily record routes, mileage, and gas expenses
  • Shared vehicle owners - track individual driver usage

With over 250 million cars on the road in the US alone, this is a large market with massive potential. Verizon, AT&T, and T-Mobile now all offer versions of OBD2 tracking devices; this helps Welio by raising consumer awareness of these types of products.

Business Model

We are a hardware-as-a-service company, so we have two obvious revenue streams:

  • Hardware sales
  • Service subscription

We will conduct analysis to decide the correct pricing structure to for both the hardware and monthly service fee. The goal is to lower the upfront cost to encourage wider adoption.

The data generated by a service like this also has tremendous value - this can be further explored at a later point.



Vision

Welio aims to be the Vivint or Nest of the automotive world.

Our long-term goal is to become a product ecosystem that can be upgraded and extended with OTA updates and additional accessories. Potential features/extensions:

  • Backup camera/sensors
  • Heads-up display
  • Infotainment/navigation accessory

There are many potential opportunities for collaborations and partnerships that will be further explored.
