Meet Welio

Welio is a dash mounted device that contains forward and rear-facing HD cameras, cellular connectivity, and a suite of sensors. It connects to a cloud platform to give your vehicle modern features.

Drowsiness Detection

Welio monitors your eyes to save lives

The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration conservatively estimates that 100,000 police-reported crashes are the direct result of driver fatigue each year. This results in an estimated 1,550 deaths, 71,000 injuries, and $12.5 billion in monetary losses.

Using the forward facing camera to detect lane departure and the rear facing camera to detect driver behavior, Welio will sound an alarm if you start to fall asleep behind the wheel.

The Business Case



The Problem(s)

Problem (pt. 1)

  • Vehicles
    • get damaged
    • are misplaced/stolen
    • need regular maintenance
  • Humans
    • aren't always great drivers
    • fall asleep behind the wheel
    • provide unreliable accounts of incidents
    • are forgetful
  • Teenagers
    • are rarely great drivers
    • often need to be monitored

Problem (pt. 2)

There are universal problems that are intrinsic to driving a vehicle, however, there is not a universal solution. Instead, solutions are fragmented across smartphone apps, proprietary in-car systems, dash cams, OBD-II trackers, security systems, and other devices/services.

These individual devices and services often have too high of a cost with regards to money, time, and/or effort relative to the benefits they provide, resulting in many people going without.





Market Opportunity

Welio combines all of the best features from these devices to create an elegant, connected, and economical platform that is greater than the sum of its parts.

There is currently no connected product on the market that integrates cameras with OBD-II, GPS, and accelerometer data for private vehicle owners. There is a real opportunity for Welio to define a new product category with its comprehensive features and smart cloud platform. It's a compelling product that will appeal to many market segments, including:

  • Car enthusiasts - access driving data compiled from the GPS, Camera, Accelerometer, and OBD-II
  • Parents - easily monitor their kids driving behavior
  • Technophiles - turns any car into a ‘smart car’
  • Safety conscious - automated crash reporting, drowsiness detection, and incident recording
  • Rideshare/commercial drivers - easily record routes, mileage, and gas expenses
  • Shared vehicle owners - track individual driver usage

This is a large market that has massive potential with over 150 million vehicles equipped with OBD-II ports in the US alone. Verizon, AT&T, and T-Mobile now all offer versions of OBD-II tracking devices; this helps Welio by raising consumer awareness of these types of products.

Business Model

We are a hardware-as-a-service company which provides us with several revenue streams:

  • Hardware sales
  • Service subscriptions
  • Personalized advertisements
  • Data sales

We will conduct analysis to decide the correct pricing structure for both the hardware and monthly service fee. The goal is to create a device with a relatively low upfront cost to get our device in as many vehicles as possible.

The data generated by Welio has significant value that can be used internally to further develop our products and/or sold to external parties. Automotive manufactures, vehicle maintenance companies, and insurance providers are a few examples of the type of organizations that would be interested to see our data.

Example: Freemium subscription supported by individualized advertisements based on vehicle data.

  • It's been nearly 5,000 miles since your last oil change - book an appointment at Pep Boys this weekend to save 20%.
  • You've driven 45,000 on your current set of tires - Michelin tires are on sale this week at Tire Kingdom.
  • Your Prius has a check engine light and needs to be serviced - do you want to schedule a visit to Marty's Auto Shop?
  • Stop by Dunkin' Doughnuts on your way home to get a free coffee with any purchase.

Technical Feasibility

Hardware

There are two parts to Welio, a wireless OBD-II transmitter and the main device that houses the cameras, sensors, etc. Welio and a typical smartphone have nearly identical hardware requirements:

  • Cameras (x2)
  • GPS
  • WiFi
  • Cellular
  • Bluetooth
  • Accelerometer
  • Microphone
  • Speaker
  • Battery

You can think of the main device as a screenless smartphone with two wide angle cameras. We will lean heavily on the work that has already been done with smartphone technology to quickly and efficiently develop our hardware. The recently announced Android Things IoT platform allows us to leverage existing Android development tools and developers.


Software

There are many platforms made specifically for IoT systems. We can use these services to efficiently develop and scale our cloud platform:

  • Microsoft Azure IoT
  • AWS IoT
  • IBM Watson IoT for Automotive
  • ThingWorx
  • wot.io

There are multiple open source projects that we may use to expedite the development of some of the features of Welio.

  • OpenCV - Computer vision algorithms - New BSD License
  • Comma.ai Open Pilot - MIT license
  • OpenXC Platform - Creative Commons Attribution International 4.0 License
  • Autoware - New BSD License

Connectivity

Cellular carriers are receptive to IoT companies

  • AT&T IoT
  • Verizon IoT

Welio will be designed to limit the amount of cellular data consumed. Video is only transmitted if an incident occurs. The locally stored video can easily be reviewed/downloaded on the users phone via an ad-hoc connection.
