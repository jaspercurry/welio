What is Welio?

Welio is a dash mounted device that contains two HD cameras and a suite of sensors. It connects to a smart cloud platform to give your vehicle modern features.

The Business Case



The Problem(s)

Problem (pt. 1)

  • Vehicles
    • get damaged
    • are misplaced/stolen
    • need regular maintenance
    • break
  • Humans
    • aren't always great drivers
    • fall asleep behind the wheel
    • provide unreliable accounts of incidents
    • are forgetful
  • Teenagers
    • are rarely great drivers
    • often need to be monitored

Problem (pt. 2)

There are universal problems that all drivers encounter, however, there is not a universal solution. Instead, solutions are fragmented across smartphone apps, dash cams, OBD2 trackers, and other devices/services.




Market Opportunity

Welio combines all of the best features from these devices to create an elegant, connected, and economical platform that is greater than the sum of its parts.

There is currently no connected product on the market that integrates cameras with OBD2, GPS, and accelerometer data for private vehicle owners. There is a real opportunity for Welio to define a new product category with its innovative features and smart cloud platform. It's a compelling product that will appeal to many market segments, including:

  • Car enthusiasts - access driving data compiled from the GPS, Camera, Accelerometer, and OBD2
  • Parents - easily monitor their kids driving behavior
  • Technophiles - turns any car into a ‘smart car’
  • Safety conscious - automated crash reporting, drowsiness detection, and incident recording
  • Rideshare/commercial drivers - easily record routes, mileage, and gas expenses
  • Shared vehicle owners - track individual driver usage

This is a large market that has massive potential with over 150 million vehicles equipped with OBD-II ports in the US alone. Verizon, AT&T, and T-Mobile now all offer versions of OBD2 tracking devices; this helps Welio by raising consumer awareness of these types of products.

Why Now?

There has never been a better time to create this product.

  • Hardware is freely available and economical

  • Cellular carriers are engaging with IoT companies
  • There are no devices like this currently on the market
  • Rising awareness of connected vehicle devices

Business Model

We are a hardware-as-a-service company, so we have two main revenue streams:

  • Hardware sales
  • Service subscriptions

We will conduct analysis to decide the correct pricing structure for both the hardware and monthly service fee. The goal is to create a device with a relatively low upfront cost as Welio is a platform for the masses. In addition to the revenue from sales and subscriptions, the data generated will have significant value - privacy and monetization details to be explored.

Example pricing structure with 1 year forecast:

  • 6,000 units sold during the crowdfunding campaign at $100 per piece (month 1 sales)
  • 44,000 units sold in the following 11 months at $150 per piece
  • $10 per month service fee

Technical Feasibility

Hardware

There are two parts to Welio, a wireless OBD2 transmitter and the main device that houses the cameras, sensors, etc. Welio and a typical smartphone have nearly identical hardware requirements:

  • Cameras (x2)
  • GPS
  • WiFi
  • Cellular
  • Bluetooth
  • Accelerometer
  • Microphone
  • Battery

You can think of the main device as a screenless smartphone with two wide angle cameras. We will lean heavily on the work that has already been done with smartphone technology to quickly and efficiently develop our hardware. The recently announced Android Things IoT platform allows us to leverage existing Android development tools and developers.


Software - IoT Platforms/Management

There are many platforms made specifically for IoT systems. We can use these services to efficiently develop and scale our cloud platform:

  • Microsoft Azure IoT
  • AWS IoT
  • IBM Watson IoT for Automotive
  • ThingWorx
  • wot.io

In addition, there are several companies that offer platforms specifically designed for connected vehicle products. There may be an opportunity for collaboration to expedite the product development and gain traction in the marketplace.

  • Mojio Open Connected Car Platform
  • Agnik Connected Vehicle Platform
  • Vinli Cloud Platform
  • Bright-Box Remoto

Connectivity

Cellular carriers are receptive to IoT companies

  • AT&T IoT
  • Verizon IoT

Welio will be designed to limit the amount of cellular data consumed. Video is only transmitted if an incident occurs. The locally stored video can easily be reviewed/downloaded on the users phone via an ad-hoc connection.
